Place names in disputed border regions are often presented very differently in Indian, Chinese, and Pakistani official maps. Each side uses the names that align with its own territorial claims, and critics sometimes describe renaming practices as political or propagandistic.

Below is a structured comparison of **commonly cited disputed locations** in:

* **Aksai Chin**
* **Ladakh**
* **Arunachal Pradesh / “Zangnan”**
* **Shaksgam Valley (Trans-Karakoram Tract)**
* **Pakistan-administered Kashmir (PoK / Gilgit-Baltistan)**
* **Wakhan Corridor (Afghanistan–China–Pakistan junction)**

> Note: Names listed are official or widely used forms by each side. Whether a change is considered “propaganda” is a political characterization rather than an objective classification.

| Common Name     | Chinese Name     |
| --------------- | ---------------- |

India does not administer or rename this region.

## Key Observations

* China has recently standardized dozens of Mandarin names for locations in Arunachal Pradesh.
* India does not recognize these names and continues to use traditional or English forms.
* Pakistan and India primarily differ in territorial terminology (PoK vs AJK).
* In Aksai Chin, China uses Tibetan/Mandarin forms; India uses historical Ladakhi/colonial survey names.
* The Wakhan Corridor is not renamed by India or Pakistan officially.

### **Arunachal Pradesh (India) – Chinese Standardised Names**

China has released *multiple batches* since **2017** of Chinese-language names for places it claims in Arunachal Pradesh, which India considers entirely Indian territory. ([The Indian Express][1])

| **Indian Name (Common English / Local)**                | **Chinese Name / Standardised Name (Pinyin/Characters)**             |
| ------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Arunachal Pradesh                                       | 藏南 (Zangnan — “South Tibet”)                                         |
| Tawang region                                           | (Included in several lists with Chinese characters for local places) |
| Sela La / Sela Pass                                     | Se La (Chinese standard name)                                        |
| Mechukha (Mechuka)                                      | (Included with specific Chinese standard name in list)               |
| Manigong                                                | (Chinese standard name given)                                        |
| Other residential villages (e.g., in West Siang, Anjaw) | Chinese-assigned residential names                                   |
| Mountain peaks (multiple names)                         | Chinese transliterations (e.g., “Wo’gyainling”, “Mila Ri”, etc.)     |

> • 6 places in 2017

## 📍 **Other Naming Issues in Disputed Territories**

### **Aksai Chin**

There is **no widely documented official list** of Chinese-renamed settlements vs. Indian names used by India, but the region on maps is shown as:

| **Indian Name / Claim** | **Chinese Usage / Reference**                    |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| Aksai Chin              | 阿克赛钦 (Akesai Qin) on Chinese maps                |
| Pangong Tso             | 班公错 (Bangong Co) on Chinese maps                 |
| Galwan, Hot Springs     | Chinese characters used with provincial variants |

This reflects differing mapping conventions rather than formal renaming lists published by China.

---

### **PoK / Gilgit-Baltistan & Shaksgam**

China and Pakistan do not primarily **rename places of each other** in these regions for propaganda; rather:

* **Pakistan** administers PoK (Azad Jammu & Kashmir / Gilgit-Baltistan) — with administrative names independent of Indian claims.
* **Shaksgam Valley (Trans-Karakoram Tract)** was **ceded by Pakistan to China** in a boundary agreement, and is referred by both as part of large administrative units (e.g., Chinese local prefectures) rather than as individually renamed localities.

There is no *public authoritative compiled list* that maps Indian names vs. **Chinese-renamed names** in these regions akin to Arunachal listings.

---

## 🧠 **Historical and Contextual Notes**

* China’s repeated issuance of lists in Arunachal Pradesh began in **2017** and has continued through **2025**, each time assigning Chinese names to varying numbers of places to reinforce claims over territory it refers to as “Zangnan” or **South Tibet**. ([The Indian Express][1])
* Pakistan’s renamings are typically *internal identity or post-colonial changes*, not *propaganda-oriented territorial claims* on Indian land.
